About

Resume (pdf): Sandra "Sam" Litschert Ph.D.

During her first career as a sailor, Sam encountered geomorphology in action: eroded sediment from disturbed hillslopes and roads washed into the cerulean waters of the Eastern Caribbean to suffocate nearby coral reefs and mangrove stands. Personally too, after 4 hurricanes and a ship wreck, the island idyll was over and it was time for a change.

Holding close the memories of those experiences, she completed an MS specializing in GIS and Remote Sensing (Forest Sciences, Colorado State University). During this time she met Dr. Lee MacDonald whose students were modeling the same eroding hillslopes on St. John. Not willing to let that coincidence float by, Sam worked with Dr. MacDonald to receive a Ph.D. in Watershed Science from CSU. Her research focus shifted westward to spatiotemporal modeling and prediction of cumulative watershed effects in forested watersheds with field work in the Sierra Nevada of California.





After graduating, Sam’s work has continued to combine spatiotemporal modeling with environmental disturbances, both riverine and hillslope, such as wildfire, forest management, development, and climate change for the Western US.

This research has always involved complex GIS development and analysis. Recently Sam has been learning about web design and development. The next step is into the world of web mapping and big data visualization ...it couldn't be more exciting!
