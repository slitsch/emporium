WebLab Portfolio

WebLab is a boot camp-style class, coordinated through FRCC and Larimer County, that teaches front end web development and UI/UX skills; I took it Summer 2014. Students spend 12 weeks and 16 contact hours/week with industry experts learning Javascript, HTML5, CSS3, JQuery, Bootstrap, Angular, and more. We worked in small groups on the Zombie App project (see below) to process through UX and UI design concepts: research, prototyping, user testing, and wireframe design.

My goal with this portfolio was to play with all elements of web design (UX/UI) and front end programming including libraries (Jquery) and frameworks (Bootstrap and Angular).

Much of the content is material that we did not specifically code in WebLab, although we covered the concepts and I'm sure there are some errant snippets.

While the design ideas are all mine, some of them involve experimenting with color and form in a way that I have not done before. I was heavily influenced by the color palettes of Wes Anderson movies, as summarised by the Laughing Squid. But, my personal leaning is towards the clean and minimalist design of the GW Emporium, my own company.

The Zombie App presentation represents the culmination of my team's work on a UX/UI project assigned during WebLab. Our mission was to research, prototype, test, and wireframe the Update and Sync database modes of the app when used by Zombie hunters in the field. I laid out the presentation template, developed several of the wireframes, and other slides' content.
